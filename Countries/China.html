Focus on Nankin

Nankin, also known as Nanjing, is the capital of Jiangsu province in eastern China. It has a rich history, having served as the capital of several Chinese dynasties. Nanjing is known for its historical landmarks, including the Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum and the Nanjing Massacre Memorial Hall.

The city is a major cultural and educational center, with numerous universities and research institutions. It also has a vibrant arts scene, with theaters, galleries, and museums showcasing both traditional and contemporary Chinese art.

Nanjing is also known for its beautiful parks and gardens, providing residents and visitors with opportunities for outdoor activities and relaxation.

Local city actions in Nankin

In Nanjing, local city actions focus on sustainable urban development, community engagement, and enhancing the quality of life for residents. Initiatives include waste management programs, green space development, and promoting local arts and culture.

The local government collaborates with community organizations and businesses to implement projects that benefit the environment and the community. These actions aim to create a cleaner, greener, and more inclusive Nanjing, fostering a sense of belonging and pride among its residents.

The Southeast University

The Southeast University (SEU) is a prestigious research university located in Nanjing, China. It is known for its strong emphasis on engineering, technology, and applied sciences. SEU has a diverse student body, with students from various parts of China and around the world.

The university is recognized for its research excellence, with numerous research institutes and centers focusing on various disciplines, including civil engineering, computer science, and environmental science.

SEU offers a wide range of undergraduate and graduate programs, making it accessible to both domestic and international students.

Transports

Nanjing has a well-developed transportation system, including an extensive metro network, buses, and high-speed trains connecting it to other major cities in China. The city is also served by Nanjing Lukou International Airport, providing both domestic and international flights.

The metro system is efficient and convenient, making it easy for residents and visitors to navigate the city. Buses complement the metro network, providing access to areas not covered by the metro.

High-speed trains connect Nanjing to cities like Shanghai, Beijing, and Hangzhou, making it a key transportation hub in eastern China.

National energy and International agreements

China is the world's largest producer and consumer of energy, with a diverse energy mix that includes coal, natural gas, hydroelectric power, nuclear energy, and renewable sources such as wind and solar power. The country has made significant investments in renewable energy technologies and aims to reduce its carbon emissions in line with international agreements.

China is a signatory to various international agreements, including the Paris Agreement, which commits countries to take action to combat climate change. The country has set ambitious targets for reducing its carbon intensity and increasing the share of non-fossil fuels in its energy consumption. it reaches "carbon neutrality" by 2060.
