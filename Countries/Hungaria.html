Hungary, located in Central Europe, is known for its rich history, cultural heritage, and stunning landscapes. The country has a population of approximately 10 million people and is famous for its thermal baths, historic architecture, and vibrant folk traditions.

Politically, Hungary is a parliamentary republic with a multi-party system. The country has a unique blend of Eastern and Western influences, reflected in its language, cuisine, and traditions. Focus on Budapest

Budapest, the capital of Hungary, is a city rich in history and culture. It is divided into two parts: Buda and Pest, separated by the Danube River. Buda is known for its historic sites, including Buda Castle and Fisherman's Bastion, while Pest is famous for its vibrant nightlife and cultural institutions.

The city is home to numerous museums, galleries, and theaters, showcasing both traditional and contemporary Hungarian art. Budapest also hosts several annual festivals, celebrating everything from music and film to gastronomy.

In addition to its cultural attractions, Budapest is known for its thermal baths, which have been used for relaxation and healing since Roman times.

Local city actions in Budapest

In Budapest, local city actions focus on sustainable urban development, cultural preservation, and enhancing the quality of life for residents. Initiatives include waste management programs, green space development, and promoting local arts and culture.

The local government collaborates with community organizations and businesses to implement projects that benefit the environment and the community. These actions aim to create a cleaner, greener, and more inclusive Budapest, fostering a sense of belonging and pride among its residents.

National Energy and International agreements

Hungary is committed to transitioning to renewable energy sources, with a focus on solar, wind, and biomass energy. The country has set ambitious targets to increase the share of renewables in its energy mix, aiming for a significant reduction in greenhouse gas emissions by 2030.

Hungary is a signatory to various international agreements related to climate change and environmental protection, including the Paris Agreement. The country has implemented policies to promote energy efficiency, reduce carbon emissions, and support sustainable development.

In addition to its domestic energy initiatives, Hungary collaborates with other countries on energy research and development projects, promoting innovation in clean energy technologies.
